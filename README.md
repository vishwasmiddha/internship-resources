One principle I strongly believe in is that gatekeeping resources is harmful. It creates an unnecessarily toxic environment that benefits no one. Most companies evaluate candidates based on their skills, relevance and company compatibility—not on how much "better" they are as compared to other candidates. (In fact, the number of hires is directly proportional to the skill of the overall batch)



Trying to limit your peers’ growth does not give you an advantage. In fact, it harms the overall reputation of the college and can reduce opportunities for everyone. A collaborative environment, where knowledge is shared openly, ultimately benefits all of us.



One last thing - since I’m in the MT1 branch and my internship season is coming up this summer, I’m also trying to build a community for MT1 students. The goal is simple: share progress, stay accountable, and help each other improve.



If done right, this kind of collective effort can significantly boost everyone’s progress over the summer.
